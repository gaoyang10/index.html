Research

Identify Genes that Selectively Kill Cancer Cells Using Boolean Implications
Cooperated with David Dill and Subarna Sinha at the Stanford System Biology Group.
Based on the breast cancer data from The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA), we've predicted a set of synthetic lethal partners of famous cancer-related genes. These partners are important because they can help us to develop more efficient cancer drugs. Specifically, we came up with 3 heuristics: mutual exclusion between the mutation and copy number alternation of two genes, Gene Ontology similarity and pathway co-membership.
My summary poster is here.

Mini-batch MCMC Methods
Cooperated with Jun Zhu at Tsinghua machine learning group.
We want to design a fast MCMC sampling method which can samples from a Bayesian posterior in a mini-batch manner. I've proposed to use a mini-batch to estimate the accept ratio in the Metropolis-Hastings Algorithm. Further, I've also explored mini-batch sampling methods exploiting gradients of the distribution.
My draft of some experiments is here.

Detect Deceptive Answers in Google QA Community
Cooperated with Edward Chang and Fangtao li at Google Natural Language Processing Group.
I developed and improved the deceptive answer prediction module in Google Baraza, a Question Answering community. We've proposed "user preference graph", a machine learning regulerization term based on user behaviours' cluster. This technique has significantly improved our detection performance.
Accepted paper: Deceptive Answer Prediction with User Preference Graph, ACL 2013 (pdf)
